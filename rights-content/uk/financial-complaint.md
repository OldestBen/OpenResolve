# Financial Complaint — Your Rights (UK)

## Applies To

Banks, building societies, credit card providers, mortgage lenders, investment firms, pension providers, energy suppliers, water companies, and other regulated financial services.

---

## Core Rules

Financial firms regulated by the **FCA** must:
- Have a complaints procedure that is **free to use**
- Acknowledge complaints within **5 business days**
- Provide a Final Response within **8 weeks** (15 business days for payment complaints)
- Tell you about your right to go to the Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS)

---

## Complaint Categories

### Banking & Credit

- **Unauthorised transactions** — bank must refund promptly unless they can prove you authorised it or were grossly negligent
- **Fraud (Authorised Push Payment)** — under the **PSR reimbursement scheme** (from October 2023), banks must reimburse APP fraud victims up to £415,000 if you weren't grossly negligent
- **Unfair charges** — overdraft fees, penalty charges; challenge under Consumer Credit Act 1974
- **Mis-sold products** — PPI (though deadline has passed), packaged accounts, credit cards with unsuitable add-ons

### Mortgages

- **Unfair terms in mortgage** — challenge via FOS
- **Arrears handling** — lenders must follow FCA rules (MCOB) and consider forbearance before repossession
- **Interest rate errors** — request detailed account statements

### Energy & Utilities

- **Billing disputes** — supplier must accept meter readings you provide
- **Estimated bills** — cannot backbill more than **12 months** of estimated charges if the error was their fault (Standard Licence Condition 21B)
- **Smart meter issues** — escalate to Ombudsman Services: Energy

---

## Escalation Path

1. **Formal written complaint** to the firm — use "formal complaint" in the subject line/heading
2. Wait for their **Final Response Letter** (or 8 weeks if none received)
3. **Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS)** — refer within **6 months** of the Final Response
   - Free to consumers
   - Can award up to **£415,000** (decisions after 1 April 2024)
   - Binding on the firm, not on you
4. **Energy / Communications Ombudsman** — for energy suppliers and telecoms (Ombudsman Services)
5. **Court** — if FOS declines jurisdiction or for very large claims

---

## Key Time Limits

| Situation | Time Limit |
|---|---|
| Refer to FOS after Final Response | 6 months from FRL date |
| FOS — general | 6 years from event, or 3 years from when you knew |
| PPI claims (closed) | Deadline was August 2019 |
| Energy backbilling | Supplier cannot backbill beyond 12 months |

---

## Useful Phrases in Your Complaint

> *"I am making a formal complaint under your complaints procedure. I require a Final Response Letter within 8 weeks, after which I will refer this matter to the Financial Ombudsman Service."*

For APP fraud:
> *"I am requesting reimbursement under the Payment Systems Regulator's Authorised Push Payment fraud reimbursement scheme, effective October 2023."*

## Evidence Checklist

- [ ] All statements and transaction records
- [ ] Original product/service agreement
- [ ] All correspondence with the firm
- [ ] Screenshots of any online interactions
- [ ] Record of phone calls (date, time, advisor name, what was said)
- [ ] Final Response Letter (for FOS referral)
- [ ] Evidence of loss or harm caused
