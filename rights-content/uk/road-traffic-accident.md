# Road Traffic Accident — Your Rights (UK)

## Key Time Limits

> **Act quickly — these deadlines are strict.**

- **Personal injury claim**: 3 years from the date of the accident (or date of knowledge of injury)
- **Vehicle damage / property**: 6 years
- **Death of a loved one**: 3 years from date of death or date of knowledge
- **Child injured**: 3 years from their 18th birthday
- **Uninsured driver claim via MIB**: 3 years (notify MIB promptly — ideally within 14 days)

---

## Immediate Steps

1. **Get a crime/accident reference number** from the police if they attended
2. **Photograph everything** — vehicle positions, damage, road conditions, injuries, road markings
3. **Exchange details** — name, address, insurer, policy number, vehicle reg
4. **Witness details** — names and numbers of anyone who saw it
5. **Dashcam footage** — secure yours and ask witnesses if they have footage immediately; it may overwrite
6. **Seek medical attention** — even if you feel OK, see a GP to document any injuries
7. **Notify your insurer** — most policies require prompt notification, even if you're not claiming

---

## If the Other Driver is Uninsured or Flees

- Report to police immediately (within 5 days for injury accidents)
- Contact the **Motor Insurers' Bureau (MIB)**: [mib.org.uk](https://www.mib.org.uk)
  - Uninsured Driver Agreement covers personal injury and property damage
  - Untraced Driver Agreement covers hit-and-run scenarios

---

## Your Compensation Rights

You may claim for:

- **General damages** — pain, suffering, loss of amenity
- **Special damages** — actual financial losses:
  - Medical expenses and treatment
  - Physiotherapy / rehabilitation
  - Loss of earnings (past and future)
  - Vehicle repair or total loss value
  - Hire car costs
  - Travel expenses to appointments

### Vehicle Damage

- You're entitled to a like-for-like courtesy car while yours is being repaired
- If it's a total loss (write-off), you're owed **pre-accident market value** — not the settlement the insurer offers without question
- Challenge low settlement offers with independent valuations (Glass's Guide, AutoTrader listings)

---

## Whiplash Reforms (Since May 2021)

For road traffic accident injuries after 31 May 2021:

- **Soft tissue injuries** (whiplash) are now handled via the **Official Injury Claim** portal: [officialinjuryclaim.org.uk](https://www.officialinjuryclaim.org.uk)
- Fixed tariff compensation amounts apply depending on duration of symptoms
- More serious injuries (fractures, psychological injury, injuries to vulnerable road users) still go through the courts

---

## Escalation Path

1. **Your insurer** — notify and request they pursue on your behalf
2. **The other driver's insurer** — make a claim directly if liability is clear
3. **MIB** — if the driver is uninsured or untraced
4. **Solicitor / personal injury lawyer** — many work on a no-win no-fee (CFA) basis
5. **Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS)** — if your own insurer is treating you unfairly
6. **Court** — small claims up to £10,000 for vehicle damage; personal injury via County Court
