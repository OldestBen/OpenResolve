# General Dispute — Your Rights (UK)

## General Principles

Even without a specific statutory regime, you have rights under:

- **Contract law** — if there's an agreement (written or verbal), both parties are bound by its terms
- **Consumer Rights Act 2015** — if you're a consumer dealing with a business
- **Limitation Act 1980** — generally **6 years** to bring a claim in contract or tort (5 years in Scotland)

---

## Before You Escalate

Most disputes can be resolved without court or formal process. Work through these steps first:

1. **Document everything** — dates, times, what was said, by whom, on what channel
2. **Write a formal letter or email** — clearly state:
   - What the issue is
   - What you want (remedy, refund, apology)
   - A reasonable deadline (14 days is standard)
   - What you'll do next if they don't respond
3. **Keep a paper trail** — save all correspondence. Do not conduct important conversations verbally only.

---

## Formal Complaint

Most organisations (especially regulated ones) have a complaints procedure. If informal contact fails:

- Mark communications clearly as **"Formal Complaint"**
- Send via email and/or recorded post for proof of delivery
- Note the date — it starts the clock on response times

---

## Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR)

Many sectors have approved ADR schemes that are free for consumers:

- **Retail** — RetailADR, Ombudsman Services
- **Financial services** — Financial Ombudsman Service
- **Energy** — Ombudsman Services: Energy
- **Telecoms** — Communications Ombudsman
- **Motor** — Motor Ombudsman
- **Travel** — ABTA, ATOL
- **Legal services** — Legal Ombudsman
- **Estate agents** — The Property Ombudsman

Check if the company is a member of an ADR scheme — they should tell you in their complaint response.

---

## Letter Before Action

Before taking a matter to court, you must send a **Letter Before Action (LBA)**:

- State the facts clearly
- State what you're claiming (money, action, apology)
- Give a deadline — usually 14 days
- State you'll start court proceedings if unresolved

> Sending an LBA without intending to go to court reduces your credibility. Only send one if you're prepared to follow through.

---

## Small Claims Court

For financial claims in England & Wales:
- **Up to £10,000** — Small Claims track (simple process, no lawyers needed usually)
- **£10,001–£25,000** — Fast track
- File online at [gov.uk/make-court-claim-for-money](https://www.gov.uk/make-court-claim-for-money)
- Court fee varies by claim size (recoverable if you win)

## Evidence Checklist

- [ ] All correspondence (emails, letters, chat logs)
- [ ] Contract or agreement (written or screenshot of terms)
- [ ] Receipts and proof of payment
- [ ] Photos or video evidence
- [ ] Record of phone calls (date, time, what was discussed)
- [ ] Witness names and contact details
- [ ] Financial loss calculations (receipts, invoices)
