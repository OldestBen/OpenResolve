# Consumer Rights — Your Rights (UK)

## The Core Law

The **Consumer Rights Act 2015** is your primary protection for goods, digital content, and services purchased in the UK.

---

## Faulty Goods

Goods must be:
- **Satisfactory quality** — free from defects, safe, durable
- **Fit for purpose** — suitable for the purpose you made clear
- **As described** — matching the description, sample, or model shown

### Your Remedies — Tiered Rights

**Within 30 days of purchase:**
- You have the **short-term right to reject** — a full refund, no repair or replacement first
- Seller cannot insist on a repair instead

**30 days to 6 months:**
- You're entitled to a **repair or replacement** (seller's choice, but must be done in reasonable time)
- If the repair/replacement fails or is not possible, you get a full refund or price reduction
- The seller must prove the goods were not faulty at sale (burden of proof reversed)

**6 months to 6 years:**
- You still have rights, but the burden shifts — you must show the fault was present at sale
- You're entitled to repair, replacement, or partial refund

> **Note:** 6 years is the limitation period under the Limitation Act 1980 (5 years in Scotland).

---

## Services

Services must be:
- Carried out with **reasonable care and skill**
- Completed in a **reasonable time** (if no time agreed)
- For a **reasonable price** (if no price agreed)

If a service is substandard:
- You can ask the trader to redo it or remedy the issue
- If they refuse or fail, you can claim a **price reduction** (up to 100% refund)

---

## Digital Content

- Same quality standards as physical goods
- Right to repair, replacement, or refund if faulty
- Automatic updates must maintain the standard

---

## Your Escalation Path

1. **Contact the seller** — in writing, stating the fault and what you want (repair, replacement, or refund)
2. **Section 75 of the Consumer Credit Act** — if you paid by credit card for items over £100, your card issuer is equally liable (very powerful)
3. **Chargeback** — for debit card / credit card payments under £100 (not a legal right, but most banks honour it)
4. **Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR)** — many sectors have approved ADR schemes:
   - Retail: Ombudsman Services, RetailADR
   - Motor vehicles: Motor Ombudsman
   - Travel: ABTA, ATOL
5. **Trading Standards** — report rogue traders at [gov.uk/report-trading-standards](https://www.gov.uk/report-trading-standards)
6. **Small Claims Court** — claims up to £10,000 in England and Wales (£5,000 in Scotland)

---

## Useful Time Limits

| Right | Time Limit |
|---|---|
| Short-term right to reject | 30 days from purchase |
| Reverse burden of proof | First 6 months |
| General claim (limitation) | 6 years (5 in Scotland) |
| Section 75 card claim | 6 years |
| Chargeback (Visa/Mastercard) | Typically 120 days from charge |

---

## Template Letter Wording

When writing to the seller, reference:

> *"Under the Consumer Rights Act 2015, I am [rejecting these goods / requesting a repair / requesting a replacement] on the grounds that the goods are not of satisfactory quality / not fit for purpose / not as described. I require [remedy] within 14 days."*
