# Insurance Claim — Your Rights (UK)

## Core Legislation

- **Insurance Act 2015** — governs your duty of disclosure and remedies for insurer breach
- **Consumer Insurance (Disclosure and Representations) Act 2012** — protects consumers from being penalised for innocent non-disclosure
- **FCA Consumer Duty** (2023) — requires insurers to deliver good outcomes for customers

---

## Making a Claim

### Your Rights During a Claim

- The insurer must **handle your claim promptly and fairly**
- They must communicate clearly and not use unnecessarily complex language
- They must keep you updated on the progress of your claim
- They must pay undisputed amounts promptly — delays in payment can be challenged

### Insurer Duties

Under the FCA's rules (ICOBS), your insurer must not:
- Reject a claim on grounds that were not clearly disclosed in your policy
- Apply unreasonable conditions not clearly stated in the policy document
- Use a claims handler who is not adequately trained

---

## If Your Claim is Rejected

Ask the insurer to provide:
1. The **specific policy clause** they're relying on to reject
2. A full explanation in writing

**Common valid grounds to challenge a rejection:**
- The exclusion clause they're citing is ambiguous (courts interpret ambiguity against the insurer)
- The exclusion wasn't brought to your attention clearly at the point of sale
- Their investigation was inadequate or rushed
- They're citing non-disclosure, but you didn't know the information was material

---

## Undervalued or Low Settlement Offers

You are not obliged to accept the first offer. To challenge:

- Obtain **independent valuations** (for property: RICS surveyor; for vehicles: Glass's Guide / AutoTrader comparables)
- Write formally disputing the valuation with evidence
- Reference the principle of **indemnity** — you're entitled to be put back in your pre-loss position

---

## Escalation Path

1. **Formal complaint to your insurer** — put it in writing, request a Final Response Letter (FRL)
2. Insurers must acknowledge complaints within **5 business days** and resolve within **8 weeks**
3. **Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS)** — once you have an FRL, or after 8 weeks, you can refer to FOS for free
   - FOS can award up to £415,000 for decisions after 1 April 2024
   - FOS decisions are binding on the insurer (not on you)
4. **Financial Services Compensation Scheme (FSCS)** — if the insurer becomes insolvent
5. **Court** — for very large claims or where FOS jurisdiction doesn't apply

---

## Time Limits

| Action | Time Limit |
|---|---|
| Complaint to FOS | 6 months from receiving FRL |
| Court claim (contract) | 6 years from breach |
| FOS jurisdiction (ongoing relationship) | 3 years from when you knew you had grounds |

## Evidence Checklist

- [ ] Policy document and schedule (at time of incident)
- [ ] All correspondence with the insurer
- [ ] Photos / video of loss or damage
- [ ] Police report (if applicable)
- [ ] Independent valuations
- [ ] Receipts for any emergency expenditure
- [ ] Proof of ownership for claimed items
- [ ] Final Response Letter from insurer (needed for FOS referral)
