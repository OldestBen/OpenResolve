# Landlord / Tenant Dispute — Your Rights (UK)

## Core Legislation

- **Housing Act 1988** — Assured Shorthold Tenancies (ASTs), section 8 and section 21 evictions
- **Landlord and Tenant Act 1985** — landlord's repair obligations
- **Tenant Fees Act 2019** — ban on most letting fees
- **Deregulation Act 2015** — deposit protection rules; retaliatory eviction protections
- **Renters (Reform) Act 2024** — abolishes section 21 "no-fault" evictions (in force from 2025/26)

---

## Deposits

### Protection Rules

- Your deposit **must** be protected in a government-approved scheme within **30 days** of receipt:
  - Deposit Protection Service (DPS)
  - mydeposits
  - Tenancy Deposit Scheme (TDS)
- You must receive **Prescribed Information** about the scheme within 30 days

### If Your Deposit Isn't Protected

- You can apply to court for a penalty of **1–3× the deposit amount**
- Your landlord cannot serve a valid section 21 notice until the deposit is protected or returned

### Disputing Deductions

- Request an itemised breakdown of any deductions in writing
- Use the dispute resolution service provided by your deposit scheme (free, evidence-based)
- Take the case to the scheme's independent adjudicator — they will assess fair wear and tear vs damage

> **Fair wear and tear** is expected deterioration from normal use — landlords cannot charge for this.

---

## Repairs

Your landlord must keep in repair:
- The structure and exterior of the property
- Heating and hot water installations
- Sanitation installations (baths, toilets, sinks)
- Gas and electrical installations

### Reporting Repairs

1. **Report in writing** (email or letter) — dated and kept
2. Allow reasonable time for response (emergency = immediate; routine = 14–28 days)
3. If no action, escalate formally

### Escalation for Repairs

1. Environmental Health at your local council — they can serve **Improvement Notices** on landlords
2. **Housing Ombudsman** (for social housing / housing associations)
3. **County Court** — you can apply for an order requiring the landlord to carry out repairs; or for damages if health is affected

---

## Illegal Eviction & Harassment

It is a **criminal offence** for your landlord to:
- Change the locks without a court order
- Remove your belongings
- Harass you to leave (cutting off utilities, entering without notice)

Report to: **local council's tenancy relations officer** or the police.

---

## Section 21 (No-Fault Eviction)

A section 21 notice is only valid if:
- Your deposit is properly protected
- You were given prescribed information
- The property has a valid **gas safety certificate**, **EPC** (rating E or above), and **electrical installation report (EICR)**
- You were given the **'How to Rent'** guide at the start of the tenancy
- The landlord has not breached retaliatory eviction rules

If any of these conditions are not met, the section 21 is invalid — challenge it.

---

## Escalation Path

1. **Letter before action** to your landlord (formal dispute)
2. **Deposit scheme adjudication** (for deposit disputes — free)
3. **Local council Environmental Health** (for disrepair and hazards)
4. **Housing Ombudsman** (social housing)
5. **County Court** — disrepair, deposit, illegal eviction damages
6. **First-tier Tribunal (Property Chamber)** — for rent increases, service charges (leasehold)
